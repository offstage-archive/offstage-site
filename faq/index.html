Is RYO Coin registered with the US SEC?

SEC EDGAR company search returns no filings for Zenza Capital, RYO Coin, or RYO Digital as of documented searches. Verify at sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch.

What is a 転換社債 (convertible bond)? How does it differ from a token presale?

A 転換社債 is a Japanese-law debt instrument that converts into equity or tokens at a future date. It is a registered security requiring disclosure under Japanese 金商法 (Financial Instruments and Exchange Act). A token presale like RYO-CHAN sold SOL for a promise of future tokens with no registered instrument or conversion terms. The difference: a bond has a legal issuer, a fixed maturity, and regulatory obligations. A presale has none of these.

Does RYO Coin trade on major exchanges?

RYO Coin trades on smaller exchanges including XT, LBank, and MEXC. It does not appear on major regulated exchanges such as Coinbase, Binance (global or JP), Kraken, or any Japan FSA-registered exchange including COINHUB.

What exchanges list RYO Coin?

Can I buy RYO Coin on COINHUB?

RYO Coin is not listed on COINHUB. COINHUB is a Japan FSA-licensed exchange, but its listed trading pairs do not include RYO Coin. Marketing that associates COINHUB with RYO does not mean the token trades on that exchange.

What is the difference between presale and bond?

A convertible bond (転換社債) is a legally registered debt instrument with a fixed maturity date, conversion terms, and issuer obligations under Japanese law. A token presale like RYO-CHAN accepts cryptocurrency (SOL) in exchange for a promise of future tokens. The presale has no registered instrument, no maturity date, no conversion terms, and no legal issuer obligation. The bond has a ceiling on what percentage of the amount converts. The presale has no such limit.

Is RYO-CHAN the same as RYO Coin?

No. RYO Coin (also called RYO Digital) is an exchange-traded token listed on XT, LBank, and MEXC. RYO-CHAN is a separate Solana token that was sold in a presale at ryochan.com. The RYO-CHAN presale wallet collected SOL from participants, but as of the latest investigation, no SPL token was ever minted and the contract address remains listed as TBD. The two are distinct products sold under a similar name, which itself creates confusion.

How do I report a crypto scam in Japan?

Japan offers several channels: (1) SESC (証券取引等監視委員会) information provision window at fsa.go.jp/sesc/watch — accepts anonymous submissions via Microsoft Forms. (2) FSA (金融庁) for crypto exchange registration complaints. (3) Consumer Affairs Agency (消費者庁) for misleading advertising under 景品表示法. (4) Local police (警察署) for criminal fraud complaints under Penal Code Article 246. (5) Japan Virtual Currency Exchange Association (JVCEA) for exchange-related issues. For cross-border cases involving Singapore entities, also contact Singapore Police.

How do I search SEC EDGAR?

Go to sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch. Enter the company name (e.g. Zenza Capital, RYO Coin, RYO Digital) in the Fast Search box. If no filings appear, the entity has not filed with the SEC as a US-registered securities issuer. EDGAR covers all US public company filings.

How do I check a Singapore company on ACRA?

Go to bizfile.gov.sg. Select Business Profile search. Enter the UEN (Unique Entity Number) shown on the bond document or marketing material. For a fee (approximately SGD 5-30) you can download the business profile showing directors, shareholders, and registration status.

How do I verify a Japan corporate number (法人番号)?

Go to houjin-bango.nta.go.jp. Enter the 13-digit 法人番号 in the search box. The site returns the company name, address, and registration details. This is a free national database maintained by the National Tax Agency (国税庁).

How do I verify a 転換社債 issuer in Japan?

Request the bond certificate and note the issuer name and any registration number. For a Singapore issuer like Zenza Capital PTE, search ACRA BizFile (bizfile.gov.sg). A legitimate convertible bond has a registered issuer, a fixed conversion period, and disclosure obligations under 金商法.
