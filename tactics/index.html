Tactics

If they tell you not to look

These patterns appear in documented holder chats and marketing around Zenza, RYO, and COINHUB. Recognizing the script matters as much as checking the registry.

When someone says do not read this site

A request to ignore primary sources is itself a warning sign. Legitimate issuers welcome verification. If you are told a website, court record, or blockchain explorer is harassment, negativity, or not to be trusted, ask why looking is forbidden.

  • Do not trust me. Check EDGAR, houjin-bango, or Solscan yourself.
  • If the only answer is loyalty, not documents, that is the technique.
  • Your friend may repeat a script without knowing. The test is records, not friendship.

Your friend is not the enemy

Promoters inside groups often believe what they repeat. The same person may push manga one week and COINHUB media the next. Ask them to check one source with you. Side by side. Website on the left, registry on the right.

Skepticism inoculation

What you hear"People ask questions, but they will understand soon."

What it doesAdmits doubt exists, then dismisses it and promises future proof so you stop asking now.

CheckAsk for the document today, not the promise tomorrow.

Isolation from warners

What you hearCritics labeled negative, toxic, or troublemakers.

What it doesTrains the group to distrust anyone who brings registry or court records.

CheckRead the warning and verify it independently. Do not debate loyalty.

Trusted insider override

What you hearA friendly promoter says the company is fine. Evidence is ignored.

What it doesUses social trust to beat documents. Common in LINE groups.

CheckOne registry search. Does the filing match the website?

Narrative pivot

What you hearYesterday: read the manga. Today: like and spread COINHUB on X.

What it doesSame promoter, new script when the issuer needs a different story.

CheckTrack the message, not the person. What changed in 48 hours?

Legitimacy theater

What you hearSEC Compliant badges, karate logos, law firm names, Imperial Hotel events.

What it doesProduction value substitutes for registration. The stage looks real. The filing may not exist.

CheckSEC EDGAR company search. CertiK database. FSA list. One minute.

Coordinated amplification

What you hearInstructions to like, repost, and spread PR as if it were news.

What it doesTurns holders into a free PR army. Paid press can look organic.

CheckIs it journalism or a paid release? Who benefits from the likes?

Artificial urgency

What you hearBuy now. Rate increases tomorrow. Deadline tonight.

What it doesPressure before you can verify. Classic across TLC, bonds, and presales.

CheckPause. Run one verification. Urgency survives facts or it does not.

Jurisdiction fog

What you hearSingapore logo, Japan office, Nevada bankruptcy, Philippines team.

What it doesScatters attention across countries so no one checks the right registry.

CheckWhich entity issued your contract? Search that jurisdiction only.

Pre-emptive discreditation

What you hearDo not read that site. Do not trust court records. Only trust headquarters.

What it doesBlocks verification before you start. This page exists to name that move in advance.

CheckIf looking is forbidden, look anyway. Then decide from documents.
