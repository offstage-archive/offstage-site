Editorial standards

Editorial standards

Every page and video is published in English, Japanese, and Tagalog.

What Offstage is

A public research record comparing marketing claims to primary documents: court filings, corporate registries, blockchain explorers, and archived web pages. No author is named. No investor personal data appears on this site.

What counts as a source

  • Government registries (ACRA, houjin-bango, Nevada SOS, FSA)
  • Court records (PACER, Clark County)
  • On-chain data (Solscan, Solana explorers)
  • Official agency databases (SEC EDGAR, CertiK Skynet)
  • Wayback Machine snapshots of issuer websites

What we do not publish

  • Unverified gossip or victim chat excerpts
  • Claims without a linked primary source
  • Personal data of bondholders or researchers
  • Identity assertions where sources do not confirm

Corrections

Pages show a last-verified date. When primary sources change, pages update.

How we verify claims

Every factual claim on this site follows the same process: identify a claim made in marketing or issuer materials, locate the corresponding primary source (registry, court filing, blockchain explorer, or archived web page), and state what the source shows. Claims are separated from interpretations. Where a source does not confirm a claim, we say so. Where multiple interpretations are possible, we state the range. We do not bridge separately true facts into unverified conclusions. Every page shows a last-verified date so readers know when sources were last checked.

  • Each claim maps to at least one primary source URL
  • Source documents are captured or archived at time of research
  • Disputed or ambiguous facts are marked with explicit caveats
  • When a source changes, the page is updated or marked as stale
  • No inference substitutes for a missing source
